The Palace Museum Responsive Website

Located in Beijing, China, the Palace Museum is one of my favorite spots in my hometown. I find the English version of its official webpage very difficult to navigate, so I decided to redesign it for better usability and accessibility.
To improve the user experience, I analyzed and identified flaws in its existing interface, created low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes for various screen sizes, and built a responsive website based on those prototypes.

Original webpage →

Redesigned webpage →

Problem Identification

  1. Consistency and standards: some of the elements are overlapped when the website is opened from devices of different sizes.
  2. Flexibility: the images on the website would not adjust according to the screen size.
  3. Aesthetic and minimalist design: the fonts are not visible on some of the image backgrounds.
  4. Help and documentation: the navigation menu design makes it difficult to locate information.
  5. The efficiency of use: the website loads very slowly and has a very long response time after I click on the interactive elements.

Accessibility

The website is not accessibly designed. The main problem is the lack of alternative text (55 linked images without alternative text), low visibility (13 contrast error and 5 very small texts), and unnecessary elements (15 empty links, 6 empty buttons, and 44 redundant links). I believe that improving text readibility and implementing alternative texts would help acheive better accessibility.
